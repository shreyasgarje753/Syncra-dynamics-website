Frequently Asked Questions

A stepper motor is a brushless DC electric motor that divides a full rotation into a number of equal steps. The motor's position can be commanded to move and hold at one of these steps without any feedback sensor, as long as the motor is carefully sized to the application.

DC stepper motors offer precise positioning control, excellent holding torque, no feedback sensors required, and reliable open-loop operation. They are ideal for applications requiring accurate positioning and repeatability.

Selection depends on torque requirements, speed needs, step resolution, voltage, and physical constraints. Our technical team can help you choose the optimal motor based on your specific application requirements.

An AC synchronous motor is an AC motor in which the rotation of the shaft is synchronized with the frequency of the supply current. The motor speed remains constant regardless of load, making it ideal for applications requiring constant speed.

Synchronous motors provide constant speed operation, high efficiency, power factor correction capabilities, and excellent performance in applications requiring precise timing or speed control.

Unlike induction motors, synchronous motors operate at a constant speed synchronized with the supply frequency and can operate at leading power factors, making them useful for power factor correction in industrial applications.
